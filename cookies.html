Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how attilano ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website or use our mobile applications.

Last Updated: January 1, 2024

1. What Are Cookies and Similar Technologies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and understand how you interact with the site.

Similar technologies include:

  • Local Storage: Data stored in your browser that persists even after you close the browser
  • Session Storage: Temporary data stored during your browsing session
  • Pixels/Tags: Small images or code snippets that track user behavior
  • SDKs: Software development kits embedded in apps that collect data
  • Fingerprinting: Collecting device characteristics to identify users

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

5.1 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies
  • Edge: Settings > Privacy & Services > Cookies
  • Opera: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies

Note: Blocking essential cookies may prevent our Services from functioning properly.

5.2 Device Settings

iOS Devices:

  • Settings > Privacy > Advertising > Reset Advertising Identifier
  • Settings > Privacy > Advertising > Limit Ad Tracking (enable)

Android Devices:

  • Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of Personalized Ads
  • Settings > Apps > [App Name] > Storage > Clear Cache

5.3 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept All: Enable all cookies
  • Decline All: Only essential cookies will be used
  • Customize: Choose which categories you allow

You can change your preferences at any time by contacting us or adjusting your browser settings.

6. Cookie Retention Periods

Cookies are stored on your device for different periods:

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Short-Term Cookies: Retained for a few hours to a few days
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device until expiration or deletion
  • Advertising Cookies: Typically retained for up to 13 months
  • Analytics Cookies: Usually retained for 12-26 months

You can manually delete cookies at any time through your browser settings.

7. Mobile App Tracking

In our mobile apps, we may use the following tracking technologies:

7.1 Device Identifiers

  • iOS (IDFA): Identifier for Advertisers - used for advertising targeting
  • Android (GAID): Google Advertising ID - used for advertising targeting
  • Device IDs: Unique identifiers assigned to your device

7.2 App Tracking Transparency (iOS 14.5+)

On iOS devices running iOS 14.5 and later, apps must request your permission before tracking you across apps and websites. You may see a prompt asking for permission to track your activity.

7.3 SDK-Based Tracking

Our apps include software development kits (SDKs) from third-party providers that may collect device and usage data. This includes:

  • Analytics SDKs for usage tracking
  • Advertising SDKs for ad delivery
  • Crash reporting SDKs for bug fixes
  • Performance monitoring SDKs

8. Do Not Track Signals

  • Respects your choices through other methods (browser settings, opt-out tools)

For comprehensive tracking control, we recommend using the management options in Section 5.
